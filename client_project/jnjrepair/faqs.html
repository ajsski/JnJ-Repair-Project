Frequently Asked Questions

We have had our share of frequent questions come up in our years of service. Here is just a few that we can answer up front and hopefully help ease your mind.

1. What kind of appliances do you repair?
We service just about everything! Central Air, Air Conditioning Units, Furnaces, Washing Machines, Dryers, Hot Water Heaters, Dishwashers, Refrigerators, Range Hoods, Sump Pumps, Disposals, Electrical and so much more!
2. What kind of air filter should I use for my furnace at home?
It really comes down to personal preference, as long as you change your filter often. The best advice I give my customers is its okay to buy the cheapest filter. As long as you change out the filter every month.
3. Why does my furnance go bump in the night?
Your furnace really shouldn't wake you up in the middle of the night. If you happen to hear a bump when the furnace kicks on, chances are you have a blockage. We can help. We do clean duct work too!
4. Why does my furnace blow cold air?
This is actually a multiple choice answer. Many furnace problems are preventable with routinely scheduled cleaning and maintenance. If you have a furnace blowing cold air, we first recommend, changing your filter. If it does not solve the problem, call in our expert to see if its a broken thermostat or just an electronic control panel issue.
5. My AC is making a noise?
Give us a quick call and describe what you hear. Is it a humming noise? Banging, buzzing, Liquid sounds like bubbling or moving water? AC noises can be irritating, but they can be a key indicator for your repairman to figure out how to fix a loud AC fast.
6. How do I maintain my HVAC system's efficiency?
To maintain the life of your new system, there are a few simple steps you can follow. HVAC system efficiency varies depending on how you take care of it. Make sure the unit is clean or debris (that means raking leaves in fall). Indoor supply vents can be cleaned with a vacuum to maintain steady airflow in the system. A programmable thermostat can help save money and energy by automatically adjusting the temperature for different times in the day.
